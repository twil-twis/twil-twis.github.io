Advanced Verb Valency

After the tense marker, you can put some more suffixes to indicate the subject and object of the verb.

VERB Valency Marker [Valency Marker]

Valency can be:

  • -r for first person (-ar)
  • -t for second person (-ut)
  • -p for third person (-ip)

If they come after a consonant, insert the given vowel when speaking (but don't write it).

Markers are the normal place/animacy markers.

For example:

sjeŧterš̗ (sjeŧ-te-r-š̗, pronounced sjẹŧteriš)

"I slept."

kâvkesrk̗p (kâv-kes-r-k̗-p, pronounced kậvkesarikip)

"I will speak to you."

ciiprn̗š̗ (ci-i-p-r-n̗š̗, pronounced ci'ipareniš̗ or ci'iparenš̗)

"It is watching us."

mor̂ûttepš̗ (mor̂ût-te-p-š̗, pronounced mor̂ûttepiš̗)

"They (singular) died."

mor̂ûttepk̗ (mor̂ût-te-p-k̗, pronounced mor̂ûttepik)

"They (singular) killed."
